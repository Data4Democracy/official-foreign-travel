Took about 7 hours to run through all files. 
Some simple measures to speed up the search: 
* cache past search results
* identify common titles and remove them before search
* restrict the patterns tried by search_by_name

In the following test, we use the scraper to go through all reports and search for names that starts with 'Hon' or 'Speaker', did try 'HON' or other prefix. It is possible that members were given other titles. A full search will take a lot more time.

The criteria for a unique match is: (i) score at least 3, AND (ii) score of first 10% higher than score of second. 
The first requirement may be a bit loose, given that a full lastname match gives a score of 4. The second might be a bit too strict.

If (i) is not fulfilled, the line is labeled as 'Missing'. If (i) holds but not (ii), it is labeled as 'Inconclusive'. If there is an exception, mostly likely due to issues with line parsing, it is labeled 'ERROR'.

Of the non-errors, the following are the most common:
1. Retired legislators travelling
2. Staff or other officials
3. Two Mike Rogers serving at the same time
4. For members in the report whose firstname is Paul (and maybe lastname starts with R), Ron Paul scores high.
5. Patrick Joseph Kennedy II vs. Joseph Patrick Kennedy III
Other causes include non-standard date format and incomplete records in the database of the legislators.
